### [1] [JWST and Gemini Observations of the Active Centaur 450P/LONEOS: Nucleus and Coma Characterizations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24260)
*Charles A. Schambeau,Michael S. P. Kelley,Maria Womack,Eva Lilly,Theodore Kareta,Sara Faggi,Olga Harrington Pinto,Marco Micheli,Dominique Bockelee-Morvan,Yanga R. Fernandez,Adam McKay,Noemi Pinilla-Alonso,Javier Licandro,Aren Beck,Geronimo L. Villanueva,James Bauer,Lori Feaga,Michael A. DiSanti,Kacper Wierzchos*

Main category: astro-ph.EP

TL;DR: 本研究结合Gemini-N和JWST数据，对半人马小行星450P/LONEOS进行了详细分析，发现其核心半径约1.8公里，表面颜色偏红，活动由CO₂气体驱动，主要由水冰结晶过程引发，而非水或CO的升华。

Motivation: 450P的轨道在1992年因土星近距离交会而显著改变，此类天体研究有助于理解外太阳系小天体的活动机制和表面演化过程。

Method: 使用Gemini-N GMOS进行光学成像观测核心大小与颜色，用JWST NIRSpec IFU Prism模式获取光谱分析气体成分和尘埃形态，结合Hapke模型和热演化模型进行拟合。

Result: 核心半径1.8 km，表面颜色偏红；尘埃产率低（~4-8 kg/s）；CO₂气体产生率6.99×10²⁴ molec./s，未检测到H₂O和CO；吸收特征显示存在水冰，含33%体积冰的大颗粒尘埃模型拟合良好。

Conclusion: 450P的活动主要由CO₂驱动，源于非晶态水冰结晶过程（140-160 K），而非水冰或CO的直接升华，表明该天体受太阳表面加工程度有限。

Abstract: Between 2019 and 2024, we used the Gemini-N and JWST observatories to conduct a detailed case study of the active Centaur 450P/LONEOS, whose orbit was significantly altered by a close Saturn encounter in 1992. Gemini-N GMOS optical images likely captured the first views of 450P's inactive nucleus, indicating a relatively small radius of $R_N = 1.8\pm0.5$ km and a surface color of $g' - i' = 1.15\pm0.09$. This places 450P on the red end of the neutral/gray Centaur population and may indicate comparatively limited solar-driven surface processing relative to other known active Centaurs. A coma developed as 450P changed its heliocentric distance, $R_H$, from 7.83 au to 7.24 au, with an estimated low dust production rate of $\sim$4-8 kg s$^{-1}$. JWST NIRSpec IFU Prism-mode spectra revealed an elongated dust morphology and a symmetric $CO_2$ gas distribution in the coma but no $H_2O$ or CO emission features, with production rates of $Q_{CO_2} = (6.99\pm0.07)\times10^{24}$ molec. s$^{-1}$, $Q_{H_2O} \leq 1.2\times10^{24}$ molec. s$^{-1}$, and $Q_{CO} \leq 5.2\times10^{24}$ molec. s$^{-1}$. Absorption features at 2.0 and 3.0 $μ$m indicate the presence of water ice, and a subtle 3.1 $μ$m feature is consistent with crystalline water ice in larger grains. A Hapke-style model dominated by large ($D_{eff.} = 5.9$ $μ$m) dust grains with a volumetric ice fraction of $f_{ice} = 33\%$ fits the spectrum. A thermal model incorporating 450P's orbital history since $\sim$1500 CE aligns with the observed onset of activity driven by $CO_2$ outgassing from amorphous water ice crystallization between 140-160 K.
