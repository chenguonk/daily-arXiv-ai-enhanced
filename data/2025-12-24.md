### [1] [The Solirad (So) as a Convenient Unit for Quoting Astronomical Irradiances for Planetary Insolations and Exoplanetary Instellations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.20126)
*Eric E. Mamajek,Jason T. Wright,Noah W. Tuchow,Patrick A. Young,Matthew A. Kenworthy,Emily A. Gilbert*

Main category: astro-ph.EP

TL;DR: 论文提出"太阳辐射"(solirad)作为太阳辐照度的新单位，定义为1 So = 1361 W m⁻²，相当于IAU 2015决议B3中定义的名义总太阳辐照度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 在天文学和行星科学文献中，用于表示"通量"、"日照"、"辐照度"等的单位常常繁琐或不一致，需要简化天文应用中的辐照度单位引用。

Method: 引入合成词"solirad"(太阳辐射)作为"名义总太阳辐照度"的缩写版本，定义1 solirad = 1 So = 1361 W m⁻²，相当于IAU 2015决议B3中定义的值。

Result: 建立了新的辐照度单位solirad，为天文应用提供了简化的辐照度引用方式，与现有的IAU标准保持一致。

Conclusion: solirad单位简化了天文文献中辐照度的表达，便于在恒星和系外行星研究中引用太阳辐照度值，与IAU标准兼容。

Abstract: Measurements of physical parameters for stars and (exo)planets are often quoted in units normalized to the Sun and/or Earth. The nominal total solar irradiance, ${S}^{\rm N}_{\odot}$, while based on a current best estimate with uncertainties, was adopted to be an exact reference value of 1361 W m$^{-2}$ by IAU 2015 Resolution B3, corresponding to ``the mean total electromagnetic energy from the Sun, integrated over all wavelengths, incident per unit area per unit time at distance 1 au''. In the planetary and exoplanetary science literature, the units employed for ``flux'', ``insolation'', ``instellation'', etc., are often cumbersome or inconsistent. To simplify the quoting of irradiance units for astronomical applications, we introduce the portmanteau solirad, short for solar irradiance, as an abbreviated version of the longer IAU term ``nominal total solar irradiance''. The solirad (So) is a unit of irradiance, where 1 solirad = 1 So = 1361 W m$^{-2}$, equivalent to the IAU nominal total solar irradiance, and to an apparent bolometric magnitude of $m_{bol}$ = -26.832 mag (per IAU 2015 Resolution B2).
