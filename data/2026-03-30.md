### [1] [TOI-7169 b: A Hot Jupiter Transiting a Metal-Poor Star](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25787)
*Joshua D. Simon,Joseph E. Rodriguez,Jhon Yana Galarza,David W. Latham,Victoria DiTomasso,Karen A. Collins,Jack Schulte,Anirudh Chiti,Samuel N. Quinn,Mohammad K. Mardini,Shubham Kanodia,Johanna K. Teske,Peter S. Ferguson,Samuel W. Yee,T. G. Tan,Khalid Alsubai,Khalid Barkaoui,Zouhair Benkhaldoun,Krzysztof Bernacki,Jaikrit Bhattacharya,Jerome P. de Leon,Sarah J. Deveny,Mark E. Everett,Izuru Fukuda,Akihiko Fukui,Michael Gillon,Arvind F. Gupta,Steve B. Wilkin*

Main category: astro-ph.EP

TL;DR: 在贫金属恒星TOI-7169中发现了一颗热木星TOI-7169 b，这是目前已知最古老、金属丰度最低的拥有凌星巨行星的恒星。

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 大多数已知行星都围绕富金属恒星运行，这使得难以确定行星形成是否存在金属丰度下限，以及贫金属环境中形成的行星性质与富金属环境有何差异。

Method: 使用TESS光度测量和地面成像观测确定轨道周期和行星半径，通过TRES光谱分析测量行星质量，并详细表征宿主恒星的金属丰度、年龄和轨道特性。

Result: 发现TOI-7169 b是一颗轨道周期3.44天、半径1.475倍木星半径、质量0.41倍木星质量的热木星，密度低至0.159 g/cm³，宿主恒星金属丰度极低([Fe/H]=-0.72)，年龄古老(12.3 Gyr)，但轨道局限于薄盘。

Conclusion: TOI-7169是目前已知最古老、金属丰度最低的拥有凌星巨行星的恒星，未来通过透射光谱研究其大气层，可为金属丰度对巨行星物理性质的影响提供重要见解。

Abstract: Most known planets are found around metal-rich host stars, which has made it difficult to determine whether a lower metallicity limit for planet formation exists and how the properties of planets born in low-metallicity environments may differ from those with metal-rich origins. We present the discovery and characterization of TOI-7169 b (TIC 372048733 b), a hot Jupiter that is orbiting a spectroscopically-confirmed metal-poor ([Fe/H] = -0.72 +/- 0.05) host star. Based on photometry from TESS and follow-up ground-based imaging, we measure an orbital period of 3.4373125 d and a planetary radius of 1.475 +/- 0.029 R_Jup. We use TRES spectroscopy to determine a mass for TOI-7169 b of 0.41 +/- 0.14 M_Jup. The planet is therefore inflated, with a low density of 0.159 +0.055/-0.054 g/cm^3. We also characterize the host star, showing that TOI-7169 is ancient (12.3 +/- 0.6 Gyr) and alpha-enhanced ([alpha/Fe] ~ 0.3), but with a Galactocentric orbit that is confined to the thin disk. TOI-7169 is perhaps the oldest and most metal-poor star currently known to host a transiting giant planet. Future transmission spectroscopy probing the atmosphere of TOI-7169 b may provide insight into the effect of metallicity on the physical properties of giant planets.
