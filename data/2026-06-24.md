Abstract: Magnetized obstacles embedded within a plasma flow generate magnetohydrodynamic structures known as Alfvén wings, which act as primary conduits for the transfer of momentum and energy between the body and the surrounding medium. Our findings can aid the interpretation of Alfvén wing signatures in observational data and enhance our understanding of how (exo)planetary magnetospheres respond to dynamic stellar wind forcing.
