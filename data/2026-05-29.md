Main category: astro-ph.EP

TL;DR: 本研究通过大量N体模拟表明，14 Herculis系统的两颗超级木星在不规则轨道上的特殊构型可通过行星-行星散射重现，但前提是系统中最初存在额外大质量天体（后被抛射），并分析了系统长期演化及未来观测前景。

Motivation: 14 Herculis系统拥有两颗轨道偏心率大、显著倾斜的超级木星，这种轨道构型在动力学上难以解释，需要探究其形成机制。

Method: 使用大规模N体模拟进行行星-行星散射的动力学演化研究。

Result: 行星-行星散射能够重现14 Her的特殊轨道构型，但要求系统中最初存在更多大质量天体（后被抛射）；当前系统存在非平凡的偏心率和倾角振荡，但强度不足以让潮汐力显著改变系统构型。

Conclusion: 被抛射行星的质量可约束系统的初始构型；未来Gaia和Roman任务的观测有望将14 Her的动力学历史置于更广泛的种群框架中理解。

Abstract: The 14 Herculis system hosts two super-Jupiters on eccentric, significantly misaligned orbits. This orbital architecture represents a dynamical puzzle that demands explanation. In this work, we reproduce the system's dynamical history and current architecture using a large suite of N-body simulations of planet-planet scattering. Our results demonstrate that planet-planet scattering is able to reproduce 14 Her's peculiar orbital architecture, but only if additional massive bodies were initially present in the system that were subsequently ejected. The mass of any such ejected planet can in turn constrain the system's initial configuration. We also analyze the present-day secular evolution of the system and conclude that while there are most likely nontrivial eccentricity and inclination oscillations currently occurring, the magnitudes of these oscillations are not strong enough to allow tidal forces to meaningfully alter the system's architecture. Finally, we discuss how forthcoming observations from future Gaia data releases and the Roman mission may situate 14 Her's dynamical history within a broader, population-level framework.
