### [1] [Atmospheric Mass Flux as a Function of Ionospheric Emission on Unmagnetized Earth](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.24004)
*P. C. Hinton,D. A. Brain,N. R. Schnepf,R. Jarvinen,J. Cessna,F. Bagenal*

Main category: astro-ph.EP

TL;DR: 研究使用RHybrid模拟无磁场类地行星与太阳风的等离子体相互作用，发现太阳离子沉积率与行星离子逃逸率相当，大气质量在10亿年内减少不到3%，表明地球磁场对大气保留可能不是必需的。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 探索无磁场类地行星的离子逃逸和太阳离子沉积过程，评估地球磁场对大气保留的必要性。

Method: 使用RHybrid（离子动力学-电子流体代码）模拟无磁场地球与太阳风的全球等离子体相互作用，改变全球电离层发射率，量化行星离子逃逸率（O⁺和H⁺）和太阳风沉积率（H⁺）。

Result: 太阳离子沉积率与行星离子逃逸率相当；在典型太阳风条件下（v_sw=400 km/s，n_sw=5 cm⁻³），大气质量在10亿年内减少不到3%。

Conclusion: 地球的固有磁场对大气保留可能不是必需的，并提出电离层发射可能随时间演化趋向净质量通量为零的临界发射率。

Abstract: We explore ion escape from, and solar ion deposition to, \hll{an unmagnetized Earth-like planet}. We use RHybrid, an ion-kinetic electron-fluid code to simulate the global plasma interaction of unmagnetized Earth with the solar wind. We vary the global ionospheric emission rate, and quantify the resultant planetary ion escape rates ($O^+$ and $H^+$) and the solar wind deposition rate ($H^+$). We use these results to compute the net mass flux to the atmosphere and find that the solar ion deposition rate could be comparable to planetary ion escape rates. For the emission rates simulated, our results show that under typical solar wind conditions ($v_{sw} = 400 \ km \ s^{-1}$, $n_{sw} = 5 \ cm^{-3}$), the mass of the atmosphere would decrease by less than 3\% over a billion years, indicating that Earth's intrinsic magnetic field may be unnecessary for retention of its atmosphere. Lastly, we present a hypothesis suggesting that ionospheric emission may evolve through time towards a critical emission rate that occurs at a net mass flux of zero.

### [2] [Seafloor Weathering and Stochastic Outgassing Unlikely to Significantly Shorten the Future Lifespan of Earth's Terrestrial Biosphere](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.24538)
*Livia Zhu,R. J. Graham,Dorian S. Abbot*

Main category: astro-ph.EP

TL;DR: 研究使用改进的气候-大陆风化模型，结合海底风化和CO₂脱气随机变化，评估地球陆地生物圈剩余寿命，发现即使考虑这些因素，寿命仍可能超过10亿年


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 先前研究认为太阳变亮会导致地球碳酸盐-硅酸盐循环通过消耗CO₂来抵消温度上升，最终使陆地植物因过热或CO₂饥饿而灭绝。大多数估计认为地球陆地生物圈剩余寿命约10亿年，但最新研究使用改进模型将其修正为16-18.6亿年。本研究旨在探讨海底风化和CO₂脱气随机变化对剩余寿命的影响

Method: 扩展先前开发的气候-大陆风化耦合模型，纳入海底风化过程和CO₂脱气率的随机变化。使用最新参数约束，分析不同情景：1）海底风化反馈强于大陆风化且占全球硅酸盐风化主要部分；2）CO₂脱气率的随机波动；3）弱海底风化和低变异性情景

Result: 如果海底风化反馈强于大陆风化且占全球硅酸盐风化主要部分，地球陆地生物圈剩余寿命可能缩短，但仍很可能超过10亿年。CO₂脱气率的随机波动如果超过过去10亿年观测到的水平，会产生显著影响。弱海底风化和低变异性情景影响较小

Conclusion: 研究初步支持地球陆地生物圈寿命延长的结论，表明这一延长寿命对可能在外行星间变化的行星参数具有鲁棒性，并指出海底风化是需要进一步研究和约束的关键过程

Abstract: Current understanding suggests that as the Sun brightens in the far future, Earth's carbonate-silicate cycle will offset increasing temperatures by drawing CO$_2$ out of the atmosphere, ultimately leading to the extinction of all terrestrial plant life via either overheating or CO$_2$ starvation. Most previous estimates put the future lifespan of Earth's terrestrial biosphere at $\sim$1 billion yr, but recent work used a new coupled climate-continental weathering model with up-to-date parameter constraints to revise this estimate upward to 1.6-1.86 billion yr. In this study, we extend the model to examine the impacts of seafloor weathering and stochastic variations in CO$_2$ outgassing rates on the remaining lifespan of Earth's terrestrial biosphere. We find that if seafloor weathering has a stronger feedback than continental weathering and accounts for a large portion of global silicate weathering, then the remaining lifespan of the terrestrial biosphere can be shortened, but a lifespan of more than 1 billion yr (Gyr) remains likely. Similarly, stochastic fluctuations in outgassing rates can have a significant impact if the size of the fluctuations exceed those observed over the last 1 billion yr. The impact of weak seafloor weathering and lower variability stochasticity are minor. Our work provisionally supports a lengthened lifespan of Earth's terrestrial biosphere, suggests robustness of this lengthened lifespan to planetary parameters that may vary among exoplanets, and identifies seafloor weathering as a key process that requires further study and constraint.
