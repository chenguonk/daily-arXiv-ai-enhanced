### [3] [Formation of the Moon and Binary Asteroids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.22375)
*Nick Gorkavyi*

TL;DR: 多撞击模型解释月球、冥卫一和双小行星的形成，无需灾难性宇宙事件。通过多次大撞击从地幔喷射物质形成月球，自然解释月球缺铁特征。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统月球形成理论（如大碰撞假说）需要单一灾难性事件，但存在解释困难。本文提出更自然的多次撞击模型，能同时解释月球、冥卫一和双小行星的形成机制。

Method: 1. 假设原始地球周围存在低质量、顺行旋转的原卫星盘；2. 通过多次大撞击从地幔喷射物质形成月球材料；3. 喷射物与顺行原卫星盘粒子碰撞，使碎片稳定在卫星轨道上。

Result: 多撞击机制高效：顺行喷射物易与顺行原卫星盘合并，而逆行喷射物落回地球。这自然解释了月球缺铁特征（铁核保留在地球内）。

Conclusion: 多撞击模型为月球、冥卫一和双小行星的形成提供了更自然、统一的解释框架，避免了单一灾难性事件假设，并能解释观测到的化学成分特征。

Abstract: The proposed multi-impact model explains the formation of the Moon, Charon, and binary asteroids without invoking catastrophic cosmic events. The main elements of the new model are as follows: a. A primordial, low-mass proto-satellite disk with prograde rotation existed around the proto-Earth. b. Most of the lunar material was ejected from Earth mantle by numerous impacts of large asteroids. This naturally explains the lunar iron deficiency. c. Collisions between ejecta and particles of the prograde proto-satellite disk stabilize the fragments on satellite orbits. We demonstrate the high efficiency of the multi-impact mechanism: ejecta on prograde orbits readily merges with the prograde proto-satellite disk, whereas retrograde ejecta falls back onto Earth.
