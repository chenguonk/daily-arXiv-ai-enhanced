### [1] [The science from asteroid sample return missions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22182)
*Elizabeth J. Tasker,Harold C. Connolly,Shogo Tachibana*

Main category: astro-ph.EP

TL;DR: 本文回顾了三次近地小行星采样返回任务（隼鸟号、隼鸟2号、OSIRIS-REx）的初步科学成果，强调了这些无污染样本对研究行星形成、地球有机物和水来源以及潜在危险小行星的重要性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 小行星样本提供了无地球污染的原始物质，有助于揭示行星形成过程、早期地球有机物和水的来源，以及评估潜在危险小行星的性质。

Method: 回顾和分析三次小行星采样返回任务（Hayabusa、Hayabusa2、OSIRIS-REx）的初步科学发现，综合实验室研究成果。

Result: 三次任务成功将小行星样本送回地球，实验室分析正在揭示行星形成、有机质输运和水的来源等关键科学问题。

Conclusion: 小行星样本返回任务对理解太阳系起源、地球生命物质来源以及防范小行星撞击风险具有重要价值。

Abstract: To date, three samples from near-Earth asteroids have been delivered to Earth by Japan's Hayabusa (2010) and Hayabusa2 (2020) missions, and the United States OSIRIS-REx mission (2023). Free from terrestrial contamination, these pristine materials provide new opportunities to investigate planetary formation processes, the delivery of organics and water to the early Earth, and the nature of potentially hazardous asteroids. As analysis of the asteroid samples proceeds in laboratories around the world, we visit each of the missions, review the initial scientific findings, and explore the value of sample return in understanding our origins and protecting our future.
